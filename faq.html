Radio hosting

What's a CDN, and how does it help my station?

A CDN, content delivery network, is a worldwide network of servers that each keep a copy of your stream. When someone tunes in, they connect to the server nearest them instead of one machine in a single country. That means less buffering, lower latency, and a stream that stays smooth even when hundreds of people listen at once. Every paid Oadio plan streams over our global CDN by default; the free plan streams from a single origin server (no CDN), which is fine for a handful of local listeners.

What is bitrate, and which should I pick?

Bitrate is how much audio data your stream sends each second, measured in kbps. Higher bitrate means richer sound but uses more bandwidth. As a rough guide: 128 kbps is clean and reliable for talk and most music, 192 kbps sounds noticeably fuller, and 256 kbps is close to CD quality. Pick the highest your audience's connections can comfortably handle.

What is AutoDJ?

AutoDJ is a built-in radio automation engine. Upload your tracks, set playlists and a schedule, and it keeps your station on air 24/7, even when you're not live in the studio. It handles crossfades, scheduled shows, and listener requests, so there's never dead air.

Which stream formats are available?

Your stream can go out in MP3, AAC and AAC+ (HE-AAC), Opus, or OGG Vorbis, whatever suits your audience and bitrate. AzuraCast powers the encoding, so on the higher plans you can even run several formats at once. We don't offer FLAC: it's lossless and far too heavy for live streaming, with no real benefit over a high-bitrate AAC or Opus stream.

What is StereoTool, and do I need it?

StereoTool is professional broadcast audio processing, the same kind of software commercial FM and DAB stations use to get that loud, punchy, consistent sound. It evens out volume differences between tracks, sharpens clarity, balances bass and treble, widens the stereo image, and adds final loudness and limiting so your station sounds polished rather than like raw uploads. It's optional: most stations sound great without it, but if you want that commercial-radio sheen, we can enable it as a paid add-on on any plan.

In-store radio

Do I need a PPL PRS "TheMusicLicence" to play this?

Not for our station. TheMusicLicence is required when you play commercial music, FM/DAB radio, Spotify, CDs, or live performances. Our station plays only royalty-free music we licence for this purpose, so as long as it's the only music in your venue, you don't need TheMusicLicence to play it. If you also run commercial radio, a TV with sound, or live music, those still need their own licence.

What do I need to play it in my venue?

We provide the stream. There's no app to download and nothing to install. You just need a stable internet connection, any device with a web browser (an old tablet behind the till, a laptop, or a smart-speaker setup), and your existing speakers. We give you a private link: open it and play.

What's included, and can I cancel?

£13.99/month per venue (ex-VAT) gets you a branded royalty-free station streaming 24/7 on our global network, run and kept online by us. Pay annually and get three months free. Cancel anytime, you keep access to the end of the period you've paid for. Spoken promos in your own AI voice are an optional add-on.

Yes. Once your build is paid for, the site, its code and content are yours, no lock-in. You can stay with us for hosting and updates, or take it elsewhere whenever you like.

Yes. We can host and maintain your site on fast, secure infrastructure, or hand it over for you to host yourself. Whichever suits you.
